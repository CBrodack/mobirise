Commissions

Please contact me using the contact form to inquire about commissioning information and rates. I compose original music, as well as provide orchestration and arranging services.

Recent Commissions:

Metropolitan Orchestra of St. Louis

Alfredo Deleon (saxophonist)

  • What is a commission?                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  The commissioning process is the chance for the composer and commissioner to have a relationship in which thoughts and ideas are shared throughout the entire project. It is not necessary to have any form of musical background to commission a work. It is simply a way for the commissioner, composer, and performers to have the chance to share ideas with one another and question artistic decisions about conception and design in the creation of a brand new artistic and impactful work.
  • When should I contact you, and how much will it cost?                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    The entire process works best when I am contacted 12 to 18 months in advance. This gives me the necessary time to conceive, sketch, write, edit, and prepare the work for the performers' rehearsals. This time gives the chance to work around busy schedules, and also to allow room for artistic discourse between the parties involved. Shorter time frames are acceptable, depending on the project's scope.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     The cost of the comission depends on length, size/instrumentation, and time frame. An extremely useful resource is published by an organization called Meet the Composer. It lays a framework for industry rates for new commissions, and is a great tool for those who have never commissioned a new work before.
  • Who owns the rights?                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      The rights of the work belong with the composer. If you commission a work, you may request specific performance rights that allow the performer/ensemble to be the only party to perform the given work for an explicit amount of time. I am always interested in attending rehearsals and performances of my own works if possible. I usually ask for a recording of the premiere performance for my own archival uses and promotional purposes.
Copyist/Engraving Work
Copyist work consists of entering scores and parts into Finale, Sibelius, or another music notation software for transposition, editing, and cuts. Pricing is calculated per final page of product. Final projects will be emailed as PDFs. Graphic scores will be priced based on the amount of detail and work involved. Additional pricing for multiple versions may apply (multiple transpositions, instrumentations, etc.)
